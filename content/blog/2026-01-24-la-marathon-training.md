## Prelude

As a teen, I started running a local 15K race. After eight years of running that distance, the longest run I had ever completed was around 11-12 miles. I attempted a 16 mile training run once but struggled and cut it short. 

I told myself this myth that my body isn't built for long running and then I proved myself somewhat wrong by running the Miami Half Marathon. After that race, I thought there's no way I could run another loop of the half. And I was right because I had only trained for a half marathon.

After completing the Ironman 70.3 in 2024, I saw big cracks in my lie to myself about not being able to run long distances. Recently, I found myself without much motivation to workout partially because life stuff was prioritized but partially because, after a few bike crashes, I felt demoralized. 

But back in October 2025, I was sitting on my couch and thought what if I try to train for something ambitious before a big life event in May 2026. I started searching and figured out the LA marathon is in March and fit perfectly into my desired timeline. I signed up immediately.

## Training Plan

I headed over to the Coros website to check out the marathon training plans. I found an acceptable [20 week plan](https://training.coros.com/schedule-plan/share?planId=453063385117016064) for beginners and immediately added it to my watch.

## Goals

This is not a race for me. My overwhelming goal is to finish and see how my body responds to the distance. Secondarily, I think that I could hit about 4 hours.

1. Finish.
2. Under 4 hours. 

## Coaching

For a long time, no goals and hubris prevented me from seeking coaching and intervention. When I posted my plan on Strava, a connection suggested that I get a coach. I've heard this dozens of times. This time I decided to listen. 

I found a running article extolling the virtues of Fredrik Zillén's paid running [course.](https://www.fredrikzillen.com/courses/efficient-running-technique) I bought it and started tweaking all the things. In simple terms, I learned to:

- rotate my shoulders in the arm swing instead of rotating my torso
- keep my body upright while keeping my foot landing beneath the body instead of in front of the body

Most importantly, I learned more of how the body assists in running efficiently. Fredrik uses excellent analogies and exercises to demonstrate the concepts. 

## Progress

I'm about 14 weeks into the plan plus the coaching. I've made great progress. When I started, I was in couch potato 🛋️ 🥔 mode. Now, I'm up to 12 mile runs and nearly 40 mile weeks. 

### Coros Data

Coros is instrumental in helping me reach my marathon goal. The generic 20 week training plan would help anyone go from couch to a marathon finish; however, Coros has also helped me understand my strengths and weaknesses as a runner as well as my running type. 

Although there are many ways to describe running styles, Coros uses "arial" and "ground". After performing the running form test, Coros says that I'm a ground runner, so I don't look springy as elite runners. 

The running form test also informed me that my balance is great but my stride ratio is subpar mainly because there's too much vertical movement in my stride. And because of my coaching, I've learned that I can reduce my vertical movement by increasing my cadence. So, anyway, that's one example of how Coros and my coaching complement each other.
