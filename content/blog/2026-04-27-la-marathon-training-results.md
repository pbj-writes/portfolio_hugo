## Reflections

- I did not finish under 4 hours, but I am happy with my time of about 4.5 hours because my beginner program did not include speed work, so I went slow and steady and kept a decent pace until about mile 18.
- When the race started, my running shorts started falling down because I packed too many gels in the pockets. 😆 I took most gels out and carried them in my hands. 
- I liked that I crossed the start line quickly after the race start. I thought it might be around 30 minutes but it was less than 5-10 minutes.
- I had two bathroom breaks. One of these breaks required me to walk for about half a mile, walk into a Whole Foods, request the bathroom code, etc. I stayed calm and collected and just accepted that this kind of thing can happen.
- I grabbed a gel around mile 14 and immediately noticed that it was a caffeinated gel. I did not train with caffeine. But I ran a few more miles, forgot, and took the gel a few miles later. Unforced error.
- My legs wore out due to the inclines and declines. I trained nearly entirely on flats. 
- At the finish, my legs were barbecue chicken. I was trotting for the last 5 miles or so. For the next few days, going down stairs was difficult. 
- To do this again, I would train differently. Add hills. Add speed work. Start a training program with more of a fitness base. And then, I believe, I could go under 4 hours.
- I struggled with fatigue and motivation in last half of the 20 week training program. Also, my legs and heels were stiff. I don't think that I did enough to adapt to the training. For instance, not enough stretching and other recovery habits.
- Overall, I don't think marathon training is for me, but I also got some feedback from Strava folks that training gets easier as you maintain and build over time, which I believe; however, I just prefer to invest more time into cycling. At the finish line, I thought never again, but after a few days I started thinking about how I could improve and optimize for the next time. And, I think, this is the beauty of endurance sports.
